You might as well rest. The bed is cold and offers no comfort. You cocoon yourself in the thin, ragged scraps of
cloth on top of the bed. Your feet dangle from the edge, a chill wind enveloping them. You spend what feels
like hours staring at the ceiling of this hovel, watching pieces of straw brush against one another and
fall to the floor.
  
An internal gnawing, the same gnawing which has plagued you for as long as you can rembember, grasps at your innards
until you finally drift into a dreamless slumber.
